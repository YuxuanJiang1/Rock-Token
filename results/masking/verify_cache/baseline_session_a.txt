We are asked to find the **smallest positive integer** $ n $ such that $ n^2 $ is divisible by **12**.

---

### Step 1: Understand the condition
We want:
$$
12 \mid n^2
$$
That is, $ n^2 $ is divisible by 12.

---

### Step 2: Prime factorization of 12
$$
12 = 2^2 \times 3
$$

So, for $ n^2 $ to be divisible by $ 2^2 \times 3 $, the prime factorization of $ n^2 $ must include at least:
- $ 2^2 $
- $ 3^1 $

But remember: $ n^2 $ is a **square**, so all exponents in its prime factorization must be **even**.

So, in $ n^2 $, the exponent of 2 must be **at least 2**, and **even** → okay, 2 is even.

But the exponent of 3 must be **at least 1**, and **even** → so the **smallest even exponent ≥ 1** is **2**.

Therefore, $ n^2 $ must have at least:
- $ 2^2 $
- $ 3^2 $

So:
$$
n^2 \text{ must be divisible by } 2^2 \times 3^2 = 4 \times 9 = 36
$$

Thus, $ n^2 $ must be divisible by 36.

But we only need $ n^2 $ divisible by 12, so we can go further.

Wait — let's be careful.

We need $ n^2 $ divisible by $ 12 = 2^2 \times 3^1 $

But since $ n^2 $ must have **even exponents** in its prime factorization, the exponent of 3 in $ n^2 $ must be **at least 2** (since 1 is odd and not allowed).

So, to satisfy $ 3 \mid n^2 $, we need $ 3^2 \mid n^2 $, because the exponent must be even and at least 1.

Therefore, $ n^2 $ must be divisible by $ 2^2 \times 3^2 = 36 $

So $ n^2 $ must be divisible by 36.
